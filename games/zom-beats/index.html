Zom – Beats

You can play Zombeats HERE
or Play it on Newgrounds

“Even the apocalypse can’t stop this party, but someone has to stop the zombies from getting in and ruining it, and that someone is you!”

Zom – Beats is my first attempt at a rythm game with the idea being that it can be both a relaxing and intense game depending on what the players skill level is and what songs they chose to play.

Prev
Next

This game was a challenge to make, and at first I had no idea how I would create the levels and have the beat match the song. Eventually I decided that the easiest and fastest way would be to record my button inputs as the song plays, export the levels and edit any inconsitencies in the song and then reimport it.

This workflow made it easy to add new songs as I would have a feeling of how the song plays while tapping out the levels, but would also have the benefit of a visual indication of how the level flows making any and all small edits in post.

I believe this was both my biggest strength and biggest weakness as while it did give me an easy way to edit levels using each songs bpm as a base to when each beat would play, my inexperience with rythm games and programming primarily for Web-gl made it such that on some systems the songs would desync from the zombies and the game would become unplayable. This became extremely clear from the comments from newgrounds, where while several people found the game too easy (possibly because of my fear of making the levels far too difficult) others found it far too hard as the game would desync.

If I am going to make another rythym game I believe the 3 main things that I should focus on would be

1) keeping the zombies and beat in sync, which i would likely try to achieve by tying the zombie movement directly to the song time rather than just spawning them at the correct point in the song

2) Having more variety in levels, making many more difficult levels which could challenge players and keep them engaged for longer

3) and finally having a level editor, which would allow players to create their own songs to play and create a community around the game where songs could be shared between players. This would also slightly alleviate issues with a lack of content as players who enjoy the game could (and likely would) be able to create much more interesting and varied levels.

overall I would say that creating Zom-beats was a great experience to learn more about the music game genre and I will definitely revisit it in the future.
