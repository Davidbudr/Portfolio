ZomBeats

Play it Here
or
Play on Newgrounds

Role:

Designer and Developer

Concept:

A simple rythm game where you defeat zombies.

Overview:

The concept behind zom-beats was simple, “create a fighting rhythm game”, to achieve this I played around with several ideas, including making the game an endless side scrolling fighting game, or 2D top down game, however I eventually decided on a fixed camera 3D level based game. This choice would later bite me due to optimisation.

The first challenge that I had to overcome with creating a rhythm game was the largest hurdle of all, creating, and syncing levels. My choices at the time were to either:  1) use the Unity animation timeline to lay out when each zombie would ‘spawn’ and run onto the hit zones or 2) create a new system which would know when the zombies are meant to be hit, and spawn them a certain amount of time before then so they would be able to reach the player on time to be hit.

I believe this was my second major mistake in this project as I decided that with the self-imposed time limit I had set, the second option would allow me to make more levels, with the option of creating a level editor further down the line in case players wished to make their own levels. This however proved to be a nightmare post release as webGL speeds seem to be very system dependant and as such much of the time the zombies and hitboxes will not match up on the players system making it, as one user put it “hard to play a rhythm game that doesn't follow any rhythm”. 

However, the production of this game was not all doom and gloom. Firstly while the current system for spawning zombies did not work out as intended, I do believe it was going in the right direction as it did make creating levels easier and with a small amount of work it would be easy to share the level creation tools with users. Secondly, although the game was heavily criticized, and rightfully so, I believe overall many who played it did have an enjoyable experience as evident by the rating on newgrounds. Overall I would not classify this game as a success but I believe that it was a useful learning experience, and for this reason I have chosen to include it within my portfolio to show my progress and growth.
